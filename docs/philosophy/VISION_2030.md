# Vision 2030 — Consent culture infrastructure

**Status:** aspirational strategy (subordinate to the Constitution)  
**Horizon:** ~2030  
**Authority order:** Constitution → Founding Thesis → this Vision → roadmaps  

> We are not trying to optimize touch.  
> We are trying to protect it — until the language of consent is ordinary adult literacy.

This document is **not** a promise of delivery dates, market size, or legal certification. It is a north star for product stewardship: what “success” means if Litmo helps define consent culture for the next half-century.

---

## 1. The world we want by 2030

By 2030, adults who need platonic, non-sexual physical connection should be able to:

1. **Name** needs and boundaries without defaulting to romance or sex scripts.  
2. **Negotiate** overlap with mutual, session-specific seals — not vibes alone.  
3. **Stop** instantly without explanation, social debt, or product punishment.  
4. **Recover** privately after hard moments without forced narrative.  
5. **Participate** across neurotypes and cultures without being pathologized or scored.  
6. **Trust the infrastructure** enough that “consent grammar” feels as normal as seatbelts — not as rare as luxury.

Litmo’s role is to be **one of the systems** that made that literacy possible — not the sole owner of human intimacy.

## 3. Cultural vision (second-order)

### 3.1 Ordinary literacy

“Can we make a snapshot?” and “Soft Signal anytime” become phrases people understand outside Litmo — the way “safe word” entered broader culture, but with stronger product enforcement and less clinical baggage.

### 3.2 Plural adoption

Communities (disability, recovery, queer and trans, elder, faith-adjacent, student, veteran) can adopt the grammar without surrendering their ethics — on-platform non-sexual adult framing remains absolute.

### 3.3 Institutional non-capture

Schools, employers, and clinics may **reference** literacy; they must not turn Litmo into surveillance, compulsory wellness, or HR theater. Partnerships that require that capture are declined.

### 3.4 Education without certification

Guided Learning and public essays raise skill. Completing modules never becomes a badge of safety or a matching unlock that implies competence.

---

## 4. Societal impact vision (third-order)

### 4.1 Reduced assumption violence

Fewer harms that begin with “I thought you wanted…” because specificity became teachable and stop became free.

### 4.2 Softness as strength in public discourse

Counter-narrative to hustle culture: careful, slow, reversible connection is adult competence — not weakness.

### 4.3 Intergenerational transfer

Young adults inherit tools that treat silence as non-consent and stop as success. Design that teaches the opposite is unconstitutional.

### 4.4 Research ethics

Any research collaboration uses minimized, consented, non-spectacular data practices. Intimate logs are not content.

### 4.5 Continuity beyond the company

If Litmo ends, merges, or changes ownership, export/delete honesty and non-sale of intimate consent corpora remain obligations.

---

## 5. What we refuse by 2030 (even if profitable)

- Public safety scores or reputation markets  
- Auto-punish moderation without human judgment  
- Soft Signal friction “for retention”  
- Monetizing isolation with streaks, FOMO, or paid unlock of stop tools  
- Training foundation models on private Soft Signal narratives by default  
- Stranger-scale discovery without safety-ops and deletion maturity  
- Colonial “one correct culture of touch” localization

## 7. Metrics that are allowed to matter

Prefer:

- dual-seal completion quality and clarity (not speed)  
- Soft Signal availability and offline success  
- user understanding of non-claims (safety is not certified)  
- successful export/delete requests within published bounds  
- accessibility task success  

Avoid as north stars:

- DAU/MAU maximization  
- session count growth  
- time-in-app  
- viral invite loops that expand stranger risk  

---

## 8. Relationship to the Constitution

If Vision 2030 and the Constitution conflict, **the Constitution wins**.

Vision may inspire roadmaps. It may not authorize:

- weakening Soft Signal;  
- inferring consent;  
- public safety scores;  
- dishonest marketing of safety or healing.  

Amendments to the Constitution follow the living process in `docs/LITMO_CONSTITUTION.md`. Vision documents may be revised more freely but must stay subordinate.

---

## 9. Closing

By 2030, success looks like this:

A person under stress can stop without performing wellness.  
Two people can name an overlap without pretending a vibe is a seal.  
A culture can talk about platonic touch without defaulting to shame or sex.  
And the infrastructure that helped is still soft enough to get out of the way.

Litmo is not trying to own human connection.

It is trying to leave the language better than we found it.
