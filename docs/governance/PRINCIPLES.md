# Litmo Protected Principles

These principles are the stable moral and product foundation of Litmo. They are not implementation preferences.

## Touch is a language

Touch is not a transaction, reward, obligation, entitlement, or proof of affection. Litmo may help people communicate about touch; it must not pressure them toward it.

## Consent is ongoing

Consent is explicit, specific, current, informed, and revocable. It may change at any time. Silence, history, trust, compatibility, verification, proximity, payment, or previous agreement are not present consent.

## Safety before growth

Litmo must not weaken safety, privacy, moderation, or consent protections to increase engagement, conversion, retention, marketability, or speed of delivery.

## Trust cannot be automated

Signals may provide context, never proof. Litmo must not claim that a score, badge, match, completed lesson, identity check, report history, or prior session establishes that a person is safe.

## Privacy is dignity

Collect the least sensitive data reasonably necessary. Explain why data exists, who can access it, how long it remains, and how it can be deleted. Private reflections must not become social currency.

## Accessibility is participation

Accessibility is not post-release polish. Core flows must account for assistive technology, sensory differences, reduced motion, large text, clear language, non-color meaning, and alternatives to haptics or audio.

## No coercive engagement

Litmo must not use streak anxiety, public rankings, scarcity pressure, manipulative notifications, shame, loss framing, or social comparison to drive use.

## Human agency remains primary

Technology may guide, clarify, remind, and acknowledge. It must not impersonate another person's consent, presence, emotions, intentions, or approval.

## Failure must be safe

When state is missing, stale, contradictory, offline, or uncertain, the product should fail closed where consent or safety is implicated and provide a clear recovery path.

## The repository remembers

Important decisions, constraints, limitations, and verification results belong in durable repository artifacts rather than private chat context.
