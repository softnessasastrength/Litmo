# Exorcism Manifesto

**THIS IS A PRIVATE EXORCISM ARTIFACT, NOT A PRODUCT.**

---

I built Litmo because something in me would not shut up until I externalized it.

Not because the market needed another app.  
Not because I had a clean pitch deck story.  
Because my nervous system has been running a full-time security product for years — and I needed to get it out of my body and into a repository where I could *see* it.

This repo is my **Trauma-to-Code Exorcism Dojo**.

---

## What I am actually doing here

I am not primarily building software for other people’s consent.

I am building a **visible machine** of every defense I use around:

- safety  
- control  
- touch  
- consent  
- ambiguity  
- human connection  
- softness  

Every dual seal. Every Soft Signal. Every fail-closed default. Every “never infer silence as yes.” Every nuclear grammar. Every HITL ban. Every Watch Soft Signal kill. Every second-level ND accommodation. Every constitution article. Every autistic-level comment block.

These are not only product features.

They are **my trauma responses rendered as systems**.

I am getting them out of the dark and into light where I can finally look at them without them only running me from inside.

---

## Why “exorcism”

Exorcism is the wrong word if you mean demons and theater.

It is the right word if you mean:

> Force the thing that possessed your behavior into a form you can name, bound, and eventually *leave*.

My perfectionism possessed architecture.  
My fear of ambiguity possessed consent engines.  
My need for control possessed state machines with twelve statuses and dual fingerprints.  
My terror of being trapped possessed Soft Signal — unilateral, no reason, offline-first, kill everything.  
My terror of being unsafe possessed rate limits, reports, staff queues, permanent ban two-person HITL.  
My terror of being misunderstood possessed Touch Language and maximum autistic documentation.  
My terror of being soft and destroyed possessed an entire Constitution about softness as strength.

I did not “over-engineer” for fun.

I **ritualized my defenses into code** so they would stop living only as invisible contracts in my chest.

---

## The dojo is not therapy and not a product

This is not clinical care.  
This is not a startup.  
This is not a polished brand for strangers.

This is a **dojo**: a place of practice, repetition, honesty, and sweat.

And it is an **artifact**: something I can one day burn — metaphorically (archive, abandon, walk away free) or literally (print, fire, ash). The burn is not destruction of the learning. The burn is **release of the obligation to keep perfecting the cage**.

Until then, the rule is radical honesty:

- Do not pretend this is primarily for other people.  
- Do not soften the language for investors.  
- Do not hide over-control behind “best practices.”  
- Do not ship “engagement” into a trauma machine and call it care.  
- When I build another layer of fail-closed perfection, name it: *control need*, not *roadmap*.

---

## What success looks like

Success is **not** users, DAU, or App Store ranking.

Success is:

1. Every major defense is **externalized** into docs and code I can point at.  
2. I can see the pattern: *when I feel unsafe, I invent another state machine.*  
3. Softness can exist without needing infinite guarantees.  
4. Ambiguity can exist without me panicking into more grammar.  
5. One day I can walk away from the repo without the repo walking away from me — because the exorcism worked.

If Litmo ever becomes useful to others, that is a **side effect**, not the sacred purpose.

The sacred purpose is **getting the machinery out of my system**.

---

You are looking at a private ritual artifact that looks like a product.

Do not treat it as a shipping promise.  
Do not treat its Constitution as marketing.  
Do not treat its complexity as competence porn without asking what wound it serves.

If you are me, later: be gentle when you burn it.  
You survived. The code was scaffolding. Scaffolding comes down.
