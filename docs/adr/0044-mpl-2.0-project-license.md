# ADR 0044: License Litmo under Mozilla Public License 2.0

- **Status:** Accepted
- **Date:** 2026-07-13

## Context

Litmo's continuity standard requires a selected software license so that contributors and future stewards can use, study, modify, and redistribute the project without depending on private permission from the founder. The repository previously reserved all rights by default, which conflicted with the stated goal of a genuinely open, forkable, stewarded project.

The license must preserve practical distribution through compiled mobile applications, including Apple's App Store, while requiring modifications to Litmo's covered source files to remain available under the same terms. The license decision does not replace Litmo's Constitution, safety invariants, contributor governance, trademark policy, privacy obligations, or platform rules.

## Decision

Except where a file or directory states otherwise, Litmo is licensed under the **Mozilla Public License Version 2.0** (SPDX identifier `MPL-2.0`). The canonical license text is stored in the repository root at `LICENSE`.

MPL-2.0 applies copyleft at the file level: recipients may combine covered files with separately licensed files, including in a larger executable, but modifications to covered files must remain available in Source Code Form under MPL-2.0 when distributed as required by the license.

Repository package metadata uses `MPL-2.0`. No noncommercial, field-of-use, or custom ethical-use restriction is added; such restrictions would make the project source-available rather than open source under the Open Source Definition.

Copyright remains with the applicable contributors. This decision does not grant rights in Litmo names, logos, or other marks beyond any rights necessarily provided by law or an explicit future trademark policy.

## Alternatives considered

### MIT or Apache License 2.0

These are permissive and adoption-friendly. They would allow modified Litmo source to be redistributed under closed terms, which does not meet the project's share-alike continuity goal. Apache-2.0's express patent grant is valuable but does not change that enclosure risk.

### GNU Affero General Public License v3.0

AGPL-3.0 provides stronger whole-program and network-use copyleft. It was not selected because its broader obligations add integration, contributor, and mobile distribution complexity that is disproportionate to the current iOS-first project.

### A custom ethical-use or noncommercial license

This could express mission-aligned restrictions directly, but it would discriminate by field of endeavor and would not be an open-source license. Litmo's safety requirements remain governed by product policy, the Constitution, stewardship, and applicable law rather than custom software-license restrictions.

### No license

Keeping all rights reserved would prevent lawful reuse, forks, and independent stewardship without case-by-case permission, directly undermining project continuity.

## Consequences

- Litmo becomes openly reusable under a recognized file-level copyleft license.
- Distributed modifications to covered files must remain available under MPL-2.0 as required by the license.
- Larger works may include covered Litmo files alongside files under other terms.
- Third-party code and dependencies retain their own licenses.
- Licensing does not certify the software as safe, production-ready, clinically reviewed, or legally compliant.
- A separate contributor policy, trademark policy, dependency-notice audit, and qualified legal review are still needed before broader public contribution and production distribution.

## Follow-up work

1. Create a contributor policy and choose a lightweight contribution attestation mechanism, such as Developer Certificate of Origin sign-off, before accepting substantial outside contributions.
2. Publish a trademark policy before encouraging third-party distributions that use Litmo's name or visual identity.
3. Audit bundled assets, generated artifacts, and third-party notices before production release.
4. Obtain qualified legal review of licensing, contributor, trademark, and distribution practices before public launch; this ADR records a founder-selected engineering/governance decision and is not legal advice.
