# Roadmap

Litmo develops in safety-first milestones rather than feature accumulation.

## Foundation

- Playable mobile vertical slice
- Canonical consent engine
- Persistent profiles and row-level security
- Session lifecycle and Consent Snapshot authority
- Durable documentation and resumable development workflow

## Security and private beta

- Passkey-first authentication
- Biometric application locking
- Sensitive-data encryption
- Consent withdrawal and emergency stop
- Privacy-safe notifications
- Invite-only two-person beta
- Real-device and TestFlight validation

## Guided learning

Short, step-by-step learning modules should teach both interface mechanics and the reasons behind Litmo's safety model. Planned topics include Touch Language, Consent Snapshots, Soft Signal, respectful communication, accessibility, privacy, and guided practice with fictional scenarios.

Learning must avoid competitive gamification, public badges, universal consent certification, and engagement-maximizing mechanics.

## Companion clients

Potential future companion clients include native Universal macOS support and a Linux-compatible web or desktop experience. Desktop clients should support preparation, learning, review, and account management without weakening the requirement for immediately available portable emergency-stop controls during real-world sessions.

## Deliberately deferred

- Open stranger discovery at scale
- Public trust or safety scores
- Engagement-ranking systems
- Payments and token systems
- Precise live-location tracking
- Broad third-party integrations
- Production AI features without a formal provider-neutral safety review

Roadmap documents preserve intent but do not automatically authorize implementation. Milestone promotion remains an explicit human decision.
