# Litmo Wiki

**Reality check:** Litmo is currently one founder's private, AI-assisted emotional containment tool for their own relationship — not a company, not a public product, and not used by anyone beyond the founder and their partner. There are no contributors, reviewers, beta testers, or maintainers today. See the repo's `CURRENT_STATE.md` and `docs/REAL_PURPOSE.md` for the canonical framing. The rest of this wiki was written in an earlier "real product" voice and is kept for the engineering/design thinking it contains, not because the product-language claims below are current.

> **Touch is not a transaction — it is a language.**

Litmo is a consent-centered, trauma-informed design for safe, non-sexual, platonic physical connection — currently practiced by one real couple, not a general platform.

This wiki explains the product philosophy, safety model, architecture, development workflow, and roadmap in a form written as if for contributors, reviewers, beta testers, and future maintainers — a writing exercise, since none of those currently exist.

## What Litmo is

Litmo helps adults describe what safe touch means to them, compare compatible boundaries, affirm a shared Consent Snapshot, and stop immediately without explanation.

Litmo is **not** a dating app, therapy service, emergency service, or certification that another person is universally safe.

## Core experience

```text
Welcome → Vibe Quiz → Vibe Profile → Touch Language
→ Discover → Match Detail → Consent Snapshot
→ Active Session → Wrap-Up → Trust Ledger
```

## Current status

Litmo remains an experimental application under active development. The repository contains a playable mobile prototype, a canonical consent engine, session-lifecycle infrastructure, security and privacy work, and extensive documentation. It is not a public product, is not production-ready, and is not intended to arrange interactions with strangers — it is currently used only by its founder and their partner.

## Governing principle

Safety logic is product logic. Consent is explicit, current, revocable, and session-specific. Trust, compatibility, prior sessions, and verification never substitute for consent.
