# Glossary

## Consent Snapshot
An immutable, session-specific representation of the strict compatible intersection of both participants' current boundaries.

## Touch Language
A structured way for a user to describe preferred touch types, pressure, duration, body zones, environment, and nervous-system context.

## Soft Signal
Litmo's immediate stop mechanism. It ends the interaction without requiring explanation, negotiation, or counterpart approval.

## Trust Ledger
A private, carefully limited history of authorized session outcomes. It provides context but never certifies universal safety.

## Fail closed
A safety posture in which missing, stale, contradictory, or unavailable information prevents activation rather than triggering a guess.

## Terminal state
A session state such as completed, exited, or cancelled that cannot be reactivated.

## RLS
Row-Level Security. PostgreSQL policies restricting which records a user may read or modify.

## ADR
Architecture Decision Record. A durable explanation of an important decision, its context, alternatives, and consequences.

## Passkey
A phishing-resistant credential based on public-key cryptography, generally protected by the user's device authentication.

## Device-owner authentication
Platform authentication such as Face ID, Touch ID, or the device passcode/password. Litmo receives only the success or failure result, not biometric data.

## Demo mode
A clearly isolated, synthetic-data experience used for founder review and physical-device testing without a live backend.

## Protected time
A product principle that notifications and engagement mechanics should not intrude on moments intentionally reserved for human connection.
