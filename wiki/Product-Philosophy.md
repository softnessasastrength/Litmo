# Product Philosophy

Litmo begins from a simple premise:

> **Touch is not a transaction — it is a language.**

The product exists to help people communicate that language with clarity, dignity, and agency.

## What follows from that premise

- Touch preferences are personal, contextual, and changeable.
- Silence, compatibility, history, or trust must never be treated as consent.
- A user must always be able to stop without explanation.
- Product convenience must never outrank safety.
- Privacy is not an optional enhancement; it is part of the product promise.
- Accessibility is part of safety.
- The system should reduce uncertainty without claiming omniscience.

## Product order

```text
Founding Thesis
      ↓
Constitution
      ↓
Product Requirements
      ↓
Architecture
      ↓
Code
```

The implementation is expected to remain accountable to the philosophy above it.

## What Litmo is not

Litmo is not:

- a dating app;
- a therapy platform;
- an emergency or crisis service;
- a universal safety certification;
- a ranking system for human worth;
- a substitute for direct communication;
- a mechanism for pressuring someone to continue.

## Design posture

The interface should feel calm, clear, warm, and non-coercive. It should avoid dark patterns, urgency manipulation, competitive gamification, public reputation scoring, and language that assigns blame when someone changes their mind.
