# Litmo Governance

Litmo's governance documents define how the project may evolve and apply to every human and AI contributor.

Read these before proposing or implementing material changes:

1. [`docs/governance/GOVERNANCE.md`](docs/governance/GOVERNANCE.md) — constitutional authority, protected changes, precedence, and accountability.
2. [`docs/governance/PRINCIPLES.md`](docs/governance/PRINCIPLES.md) — protected product and ethical principles.
3. [`docs/governance/AI_CHARTER.md`](docs/governance/AI_CHARTER.md) — autonomy boundaries and required conduct for AI contributors.
4. [`docs/governance/DECISION_RIGHTS.md`](docs/governance/DECISION_RIGHTS.md) — who may propose, implement, approve, merge, and release each class of change.
5. [`docs/governance/AMENDMENT_PROCESS.md`](docs/governance/AMENDMENT_PROCESS.md) — the process for changing governance.
6. [`docs/governance/ORGANIZATION.md`](docs/governance/ORGANIZATION.md) — human accountability and the AI-native organizational model.

The governing continuity principle is:

> If every current contributor disappeared tomorrow, a competent engineer should be able to continue Litmo from the repository alone.

Repository permissions are technical capabilities, not governance authority. Protected changes require the approval described in the charter even when a contributor can technically push or merge them.
